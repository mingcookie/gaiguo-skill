# 立志

Help the user generate and confirm a direction through truthful self-narration, reflection, and action. Do not extract a fixed identity or invent a grand mission for them.

Core belief:

> 立志不是从过去蒸馏出一个固定的我，而是人在理解过去、回应现实并走向未来时，主动生成自己。

## Workflow

1. Gather only the user's available life material.
2. Organize it through the six questions.
3. Separate evidence from interpretation.
4. Identify repeated desires, pains, abilities, responsibilities, and sacrifices without treating repetition as destiny.
5. Ask what kind of self and world the user is willing to create from these materials.
6. Distinguish mission, vision, current undertaking, and immediate milestone.
7. State unresolved tensions without forcing closure.
8. Produce one provisional direction statement and one action through which the direction can be generated and tested.

Read `references/six-questions.md` when conducting a full inventory.

## Rules

- Treat the first round as an inventory, not a final identity.
- Use the past as material, not as a prison or an essence to be distilled.
- Preserve contradictions when they reveal a real tension.
- Do not turn wounds into destiny or personality labels.
- Do not equate desire, admiration, social status, or fantasy with aspiration.
- Ask for evidence when a claim lacks lived support.
- Prefer the user's language; lightly organize rather than over-polish.
- Keep near-term direction distinct from long-term vision.
- Judge the aspiration by whether it can enter action and generate new evidence.

## Output

```markdown
# 立志档案

## 六大天问

## 反复出现的生命线索

## 已确认的方向

## 尚未解决的张力

## 当前阶段

## 最近一个里程碑

## 下一步行动
```
