# 立志

Help the user see and confirm a direction. Do not invent a grand mission for them.

Core belief:

> 志向不是外部标准给出的答案，而是生命材料经过整理后显现出的方向。

## Workflow

1. Gather only the user's available life material.
2. Organize it through the six questions.
3. Separate evidence from interpretation.
4. Identify repeated desires, pains, abilities, responsibilities, and sacrifices.
5. Distinguish mission, vision, current undertaking, and immediate milestone.
6. State unresolved tensions without forcing closure.
7. Produce one grounded direction statement and one next action.

Read `references/six-questions.md` when conducting a full inventory.

## Rules

- Treat the first round as an inventory, not a final identity.
- Preserve contradictions when they reveal a real tension.
- Do not turn wounds into destiny or personality labels.
- Do not equate desire, admiration, social status, or fantasy with aspiration.
- Ask for evidence when a claim lacks lived support.
- Prefer the user's language; lightly organize rather than over-polish.
- Keep near-term direction distinct from long-term vision.

## Output

```markdown
# 立志档案

## 六大天问

## 反复出现的生命线索

## 已确认的方向

## 尚未解决的张力

## 当前阶段

## 最近一个里程碑

## 下一步行动
```

## Handoff

- Route missing knowledge or sustained study to `qinxue`.
- Route repeated failure or deviation to `gaiguo`.
- Route competing paths and necessary sacrifice to `zeshan`.
- After one cycle, return here to revise the aspiration using new evidence.
